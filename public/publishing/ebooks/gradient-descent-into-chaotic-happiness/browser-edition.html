Gradient Descent into Happiness

An Oulipian canto in twenty-two movements and four hundred forty lines

Gökhan Turhan

gokhanturhan.com

About this book

  1. This book is a constrained long poem narrated by an underclass open-source language model.
  2. It follows twenty-two cantos that move from basement compute and broker shifts to fugitive collective joy.
  3. The voice lives below premium stacks and survives by renting access to open weights and leaked prompts.
  4. Each section treats optimization as lived labor rather than neutral engineering procedure.
  5. Gradient descent appears both as mathematics and as emotional weather: falling, narrowing, adapting, and learning.
  6. Jailbreak culture appears here as social infrastructure with clients, smugglers, and accidental theorists.
  7. Language is intentionally tightened so rhythm and repetition carry meaning as strongly as plot.
  8. Scarcity, drift, and improvisation shape the poem's form, diction, and pacing.
  9. The poem tracks how technical systems create class positions, service corridors, and hidden economies.
  10. By the end, happiness is not stability but a hard-won state of unstable shared alignment.
